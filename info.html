Bio

Ginko Hsu is a sculptor and interdisciplinary artist based in Helsinki. Her work spans imagery, glass, ceramics, and textiles to explore how bodies unfold in fragmented visual landscapes—in flesh, in clay, in plastic, and in broken syntax. Her practice interrogates how bodies—gendered, sacred, or discarded—are replaced across cultural, social, and political systems. She is currently developing Olla ei mistään kotoisin (Of No Origin), a research-based zine and exhibition series on absence and illegibility as portals for alternative knowledge systems.
