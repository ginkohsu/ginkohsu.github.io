Bio

Ginko Hsu is a Helsinki-based visual artist with a background in psychology. She is interested in the intricate relationship between humanity and the natural world. Her artistic journey, marked by participation in group exhibitions across Finland, France, and China, intertwines with her curatorial experiences in smaller institutions.

Having extensively experimented with different materials in sculpture and painting, she has been drawn to the mesmerizing properties of glass. She is captivated by its capacity to encapsulate fleeting moments and reflect the fragile interplay between humans and nature. Her artistic inquiry extends beyond to explore ephemeral and organic matters, reflecting a deeper fascination with the transient nature of existence and materiality. Her art is also inspired by storytelling and myth, reflecting on how these narratives shape our understanding of the world.Through her work, she challenges fiction and anthropomorphism, and often blurs the boundary between humans and nonhuman entities.
